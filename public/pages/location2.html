Hilltop City
A small city hides this little treasure : our first help center.
A lot of people visit us every year

Matino rises in the Salento greenhouses at 75 m s.l.m. 10 km from the Ionian coast and 25 km from the extreme tip of the Salento peninsula represented by Capo di Leuca. The territory, strongly karst, is free of surface waterways but very rich in underground waterways fed by the so-called "Vore", natural swallows of the rainwater located in natural depressions of the ground.

The city dominates the valley called Taviano-Matino which is one of the most fertile areas of Salento. The predominant crops are the olive and the vine but with large areas dedicated to arable crops and greenhouse crops, especially flowers. Very frequent are farms, large companies that insist on large estates, today, except in some cases of reuse in key agritourism. The so-called caseddhi, or pajare (haystacks), typical example of rural buildings, derived from ancient building techniques of magnogreca origin, are quite widespread. Widespread the rural building of modern type as evidence of an attachment to the land of the places and a lively agritourism market.
